# Memora — The Founder's Brain 🧠

> Your company's institutional memory. Persists across every session. Organises itself overnight. Gets sharper the more you feed it.

---

**Memora** turns Claude into a founder-grade intelligence layer built on plain-text files you own. Your decisions, your market signals, your GTM rationale, your investor conversations — all of it remembered, cross-linked, and ready to reason from the moment you open a chat.

No re-briefing. No context loss. No starting from scratch.

---

## The problem every founder has

You re-brief people constantly.

Your CTO. Your first sales hire. A new advisor. A VC on their third meeting. You spend hours reconstructing context that already exists inside your head — because there's nowhere for it to live except there.

Then you open Claude. And you do it again.

Every session starts cold. Every time you close the chat, the context dies. You're the single point of failure for your own company's memory.

Memora fixes this. Your vault holds everything. Claude reads it before answering. You stop re-briefing and start building.

## What Memora can do — as a founder

### Your context loads before you type a word

Run `01-founder-profile.md` once. Claude interviews you across 9 questions — your company stage, ICP, GTM motion, north star metric, active ventures, fundraise status, competitors. It writes all of it into `CLAUDE.md`.

From that moment, every Claude session opens with full context already loaded. No introduction. No "let me give you some background." You open the chat and go straight to the problem.

---

### Investor and board prep in minutes, not hours

Board call on Friday. You say: **"run the board-prep playbook."**

Claude reads your last 30 days of Outputs and Feedback, pulls your north star metric, surfaces what shipped and what stalled, and hands you a board-ready narrative. You edit for 10 minutes instead of spending 3 hours reconstructing what actually happened.

Same for investor updates. One phrase. Draft from your real metrics. Done.

---

### Competitor intelligence that compounds

Every time you read a competitor's pricing page, see a feature drop, or hear something in a podcast — drop it in Inputs.

Claude files it under that competitor, cross-links it to your positioning and ICP pages, and flags: *"this changes your differentiation story in the enterprise segment."*

Six months in, you have a living competitive map — not a deck that went stale in February.

---

### Your GTM motion, documented and queryable

Your sales motion, your ICP definition, your objection-handling — all of it in the vault.

When you hire your first sales rep, you don't scramble to write a playbook. You say "pull everything we know about our ICP and top objections" and Memora hands them a brief. Onboarding that used to take weeks takes days.

---

### A morning brief before you open a single tab

The daily automation runs at 7am. By the time you pick up your phone:

- Anything dropped in overnight is filed and cross-linked
- Stale notes and broken links are flagged
- You get a 3-line brief: what came in, what needs resolving, the one thing in the vault that matters most given your current goals

---

### A weekly debrief that finds patterns you miss

Every Friday: **"run the weekly-debrief playbook."**

Claude reads all your Feedback and Outputs from the week, checks your north star metric, and writes a founder debrief — what shipped, what stalled (honestly), key signals captured, and the one decision you've been avoiding.

After 4 weeks it starts surfacing patterns across debriefs. The thing you keep saying will happen next week. The metric that keeps slipping for the same reason. You can't unsee it once it names it.

---

### Any repeated task becomes one phrase

Your investor update format. Your hiring brief structure. Your customer discovery call summary.

Do it once, build a playbook from `03-build-playbook.md`. Next time: one phrase, Claude runs the whole workflow from your actual vault data in under a minute.

## Setup

### 1. Install Claude Desktop

Download from `claude.com/download`, install, and sign in. You need a paid plan. Open the app and click the **Code** tab.

### 2. Install Obsidian and create your vault

Download from `obsidian.md` (free). Click **Create new vault**, name it `memora`, pick a folder, and click Create.

### 3. Connect Obsidian to Claude

In Obsidian: **Settings → Community plugins → Turn on community plugins → Browse → search "Local REST API" → Install → Enable.**

Open the plugin settings and copy the API Key.

**Modern method (recommended):**

```bash
claude mcp add --transport http obsidian https://127.0.0.1:27124/mcp/ --header "Authorization: Bearer YOUR-API-KEY"
```

If you get a certificate warning, switch to HTTP:

```bash
claude mcp add --transport http obsidian http://127.0.0.1:27123/mcp/ --header "Authorization: Bearer YOUR-API-KEY"
```

**Classic method (fallback, requires Node.js or uv):**

```bash
claude mcp add-json obsidian-vault '{ "command": "uvx", "args": ["mcp-obsidian"], "env": { "OBSIDIAN_API_KEY": "YOUR-API-KEY", "OBSIDIAN_HOST": "127.0.0.1", "OBSIDIAN_PORT": "27124" } }'
```

**Test it:** In Claude Code, type: `list every file in my Obsidian vault`. If it reads your notes back, you're connected.

---

### 4. Build your founder profile

Paste the prompt from `prompts/01-founder-profile.md` into Claude Code. Claude interviews you once across 9 questions and writes your full profile into `CLAUDE.md` at the vault root.

From that moment, every session starts with full context.

---

### 5. Set up your first venture

Each initiative gets its own memory. Use `prompts/02-build-venture.md`. You get a clean pipeline:

- **Inputs** — raw material lands here (notes, calls, articles, research)
- **Process** — Claude works here (drafts, analysis, synthesis)
- **Outputs** — finished work goes here (decks, memos, messages ready to send)
- **Feedback** — results and metrics live here (what worked, what didn't, what to change next)

---

### 6. Build playbooks for everything you repeat

Investor update. Pipeline review. Competitive teardown. Hiring brief.

Use `prompts/03-build-playbook.md`. Show Claude how you do it once. After that, one phrase runs the whole thing.

---

### 7. Wire in live data (optional)

```bash
claude mcp add google-workspace uvx workspace-mcp --tools calendar
```

Add Gmail, Slack, or Notion the same way. Grant read-only access wherever you can.

---

### 8. Put it on autopilot

In Claude Desktop: **Schedule tab → + New task → Daily 7:00am.**

Paste the prompt from `prompts/04-morning-intel.md`. You wake up to an organised vault and a 3-line brief every morning.

## The one rule you never break

Telling an agent "don't delete this" is a wish, not a setting. Connecting Claude to your vault grants full read/write/delete access — a real data-loss risk.

- Keep a private GitHub backup of your vault (version history + sync, for free)
- Grant read-only, scoped access wherever the workflow allows
- Never put secrets, API keys, or passwords inside notes the agent can read

A boundary enforced by permissions is real. A boundary written in a prompt is not.

## Why this beats one long chat

A long chat holds context until it collapses: it slows down, burns tokens re-reading itself on every reply, and loses everything when you close the window.

Memora holds context in plain-text files you own. It only pulls what's relevant into each session. One gets slower and more expensive as it grows. The other gets sharper.

This also isn't search over a pile of notes (RAG). It's a structured, pre-reasoned set of pages Claude is meant to reason *from* — not dig through at query time. That's why answers improve as the vault grows.
