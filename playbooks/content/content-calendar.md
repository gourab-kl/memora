# Playbook: content-calendar

**Trigger:** "run the content-calendar playbook for [month or time period]"

**When to use:** At the start of each month, or when you need to plan content 4 weeks ahead.

---

## What it does

1. Read the ICP, positioning, GTM motion, and key differentiators from `CLAUDE.md`.
2. Read competitor notes to understand what angles they're overusing (so we avoid them).
3. Read the last 4 weekly debriefs and any existing content Outputs for signals on what's resonating.
4. Read any market signals, customer call notes, or industry observations captured in Inputs this month.
5. Generate a 4-week content calendar across the formats you use (LinkedIn, newsletter, or both — based on what's in CLAUDE.md or stated in the trigger).

   For each piece of content:
   - **Week:** When to publish
   - **Format:** LinkedIn post / newsletter / thread
   - **Angle:** The specific hook — a counterintuitive claim, a story from the vault, a pattern observed
   - **Source:** Where in the vault this insight comes from
   - **ICP relevance:** Why this specific topic matters to the ICP at this stage of their journey (problem-aware, solution-aware, evaluating options)
   - **Differentiation tie-in:** How this angle reinforces what makes us different from competitors

6. Flag which 2 pieces in the calendar are the highest-value — the ones most likely to attract the right audience and convert.
7. Identify one topic that's been in the vault for months but never turned into content — and schedule it.
8. Save to `Process/content/calendar-[month].md`

**Output format:** `Process/content/calendar-YYYY-MM.md`

**Notes:**
- A content calendar is a commitment, not a wishlist. If 4 weeks is too much, do 2.
- The best content comes from what's already in the vault — customer language, lessons from deals, competitive observations. Don't invent angles from nothing.
- Consistency beats creativity. Publishing something solid every week beats publishing something brilliant once a month.
