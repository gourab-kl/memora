# Playbook: linkedin-from-insight

**Trigger:** "run the linkedin-from-insight playbook about [topic or insight]"

**When to use:** When you have a sharp observation, a counterintuitive take, or a lesson from something that just happened — and you want to turn it into a LinkedIn post before it fades.

---

## What it does

1. Read the tone of voice, company positioning, and ICP from `CLAUDE.md`.
2. Read any related vault pages that give the insight more depth — customer call notes, deal autopsies, competitive notes, weekly debriefs.
3. Ask (if not already given): What's the insight? What happened that made you think of it? Who is it for?
4. Write three LinkedIn post variants:

   **Variant 1 — The strong take:**
   Opens with a single declarative sentence that most people in the industry would instinctively push back on. Then proves it. No hedge. No "it depends." Ends with one question that invites the right people to disagree in the comments.

   **Variant 2 — The story:**
   Opens with a specific moment — a customer call, a deal, a meeting. What happened, what you noticed, what it means. The lesson lands at the end, not the beginning.

   **Variant 3 — The pattern:**
   "I've noticed [X] across [N] [customers / founders / deals]..." — surfaces a pattern from the vault. Specific, not generic. Ends with the implication.

5. For each variant: keep it under 250 words. No hashtags. No "What do you think?" as the close. No bullet lists with emojis.
6. Mark which variant is strongest and why.
7. Save to `Process/content/linkedin-[date].md`

**Output format:** `Process/content/linkedin-YYYY-MM-DD.md`

**Rules:**
- The best LinkedIn posts teach something specific, not something everyone already knows.
- If the insight could have been written by anyone in your industry, it's not sharp enough.
- Write in the founder's voice, not a brand voice. First person, direct, no corporate language.
