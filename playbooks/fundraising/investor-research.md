# Playbook: investor-research

**Trigger:** "run the investor-research playbook for [firm name] / [partner name]"

**When to use:** 24 hours before any VC meeting — first meeting or follow-up.

---

## What it does

1. Read your fundraise notes, current deck context, north star metric, and company stage from `CLAUDE.md` and any fundraise venture folder.
2. Search the vault for any existing notes on this firm or partner.
3. Based on what's in the vault and what you've told Claude about this investor, reason through:
   - Their known investment thesis and stage focus
   - Portfolio companies that are adjacent to yours (potential conflicts or proof points)
   - What a partner at this firm typically cares about at your stage
   - Any public signals — blog posts, tweets, interviews — that reveal their current thinking
4. Write a pre-meeting brief covering:
   - **Their lens:** what they're optimizing for right now, what they've been saying publicly
   - **Likely concerns about us:** based on our stage, metrics, and market — what will they push on
   - **The right angle to lead with:** given their thesis, which part of our story lands hardest
   - **Questions to ask them:** 3 specific questions that will tell you if they're the right partner, not just if they'll write a check
   - **What a yes looks like from them:** what they'd need to see to move to the next step
5. Save to `Process/fundraise/[firm]-[date].md` and link to the fundraise venture folder.

**Output format:** `Process/fundraise/[firm]-YYYY-MM-DD.md`

**Notes:**
- The goal is not to impress them. The goal is to figure out if they're the right investor for this stage of the company.
- Prepare for the hardest question they're likely to ask. Do not walk in without an answer to it.
- If you have a warm intro, note who made it and why — that context changes how to open the conversation.
