# Playbook: onboarding-brief

**Trigger:** "run the onboarding-brief playbook for [person name], joining as [role]"

**When to use:** The day someone accepts an offer. Have this ready before their first day.

---

## What it does

1. Read the full vault — company profile, active ventures, ICP, GTM motion, competitors, open deals, recent Outputs — from `CLAUDE.md` and all venture folders relevant to this person's function.
2. Based on the role, determine which parts of the vault are directly relevant to their work (a sales hire needs ICP, competitors, objections, and deal notes; an engineer needs product context, release notes, and roadmap).
3. Write a first-30-days brief structured as:

   **Who we are and what we're building:** The honest version, not the pitch deck version. What stage we're at, what's working, what's not, and why now is an interesting time to join.

   **Your role and why it exists:** The specific problem this hire solves. What success looks like at 30 days, 60 days, and 90 days — concrete outcomes, not activities.

   **The people:** Who they'll work with most closely, how decisions get made, how to get unblocked fast.

   **The context they need to hit the ground running:**
   - For sales hires: ICP, top 3 objections and how we handle them, open pipeline summary, competitors
   - For product hires: current roadmap focus, top customer pain points, how we prioritize
   - For marketing hires: GTM motion, content strategy, ICP, what's worked and what hasn't
   - For operations/finance hires: current metrics, key vendors, biggest process gaps

   **The first week:** 5 specific things to do in week one that will give them the fastest accurate picture of the company.

   **What to ask, who to ask:** Explicitly name who knows what. New hires waste weeks figuring out who to go to.

4. Save to `Outputs/onboarding/[name]-[date].md` and link to the relevant venture pages.

**Output format:** `Outputs/onboarding/[name]-YYYY-MM-DD.md`

**Notes:**
- A good onboarding brief is the difference between someone being effective in 2 weeks vs. 2 months.
- Be honest about what's broken or unresolved. A new hire who discovers problems on their own in week 2 loses trust fast.
- Give them this brief before day one so they can read it over the weekend. The first day should be conversation, not information dump.
