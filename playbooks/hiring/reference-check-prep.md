# Playbook: reference-check-prep

**Trigger:** "run the reference-check-prep playbook for [candidate name] applying for [role]"

**When to use:** When a candidate reaches the final stage and you're about to call their references.

---

## What it does

1. Read the candidate's interview notes and scorecard from `Process/hiring/` for this role.
2. Read the job description and the 5 dimensions from the scorecard.
3. Identify the 2-3 areas where the candidate was strong in interviews and needs validation, and the 1-2 areas where there was any doubt or vagueness.
4. Write a reference check guide structured as:

   **Opening framing to use on the call:**
   A specific way to open that gets the reference out of "I'll say nice things because they asked me" mode and into genuine reflection mode.

   **5 questions to ask every reference:**
   Questions that are specific to this role and this candidate's interview performance. Not generic questions — questions designed to either confirm the strengths or probe the doubts.

   **The question that reveals the most:**
   One question that almost always gets past the polished reference and surfaces something real. Included with what to listen for in the response.

   **Questions for the doubt areas:**
   Tailored questions for the specific dimensions where the candidate was less convincing in interviews.

   **The signal question:**
   "Would you hire them again for this type of role?" — and importantly, what hesitation or qualification in the answer means.

   **What to listen for:**
   Specific language patterns that signal a lukewarm reference even when the words sound positive (long pauses before answering, pivoting to talking about context instead of the person, emphasising effort over results).

5. Save to `Process/hiring/[candidate]-references-[date].md` and link to the interview notes.

**Output format:** `Process/hiring/[candidate]-references-YYYY-MM-DD.md`

**Notes:**
- Call the reference. Don't email. You lose 80% of the signal in written responses.
- The references they give you will say positive things. The questions are designed to find truth inside the positive framing.
- If a reference says "I'd hire them again" without hesitation, that's a strong signal. If they say "it depends on the role" — ask what it depends on.
