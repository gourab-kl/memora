# Playbook: industry-signal

**Trigger:** "run the industry-signal playbook" (after dropping the report or source into Inputs)

**When to use:** When you've read an industry report, VC market map, research paper, analyst note, or major trade publication piece that's relevant to your space.

---

## What it does

1. Read the new source from the Inputs folder.
2. Read your ICP, positioning, north star metric, and competitor notes from `CLAUDE.md` and the vault.
3. Filter aggressively — most industry reports are 80% information you don't need. Extract only what's relevant to:
   - Your specific category and buyer
   - Your current GTM motion and stage
   - The competitors you're tracking
   - Your current quarter's goals
4. Write an industry signal brief structured as:

   **The headline finding:**
   The one thing from this source that matters most to you, right now, at your stage. One sentence.

   **What it means for our ICP:**
   Is the pain we solve getting bigger, smaller, or shifting? Is our buyer's world changing in a way that makes our timing better or worse?

   **What it means for our positioning:**
   Does this report validate our thesis, contradict it, or introduce a new angle we should own?

   **What it means for competitors:**
   Are they positioned to benefit from this trend more than us? Less? Is there a gap this creates that we should move into?

   **What to do with it:**
   One of four options — act on it now / watch it / add it to the vault and revisit in 90 days / discard. With reasoning.

5. Cross-link to relevant competitor pages, ICP notes, and the active venture it most affects.
6. Update the index with the new source.
7. Save the brief to `Outputs/intelligence/[source-name]-[date].md`

**Output format:** `Outputs/intelligence/[source]-YYYY-MM-DD.md`

**Notes:**
- Intelligence is only useful if it changes a decision. If you can't name a decision this would change, it's interesting, not actionable.
- Most market reports confirm what you already know. The valuable part is the 10% that surprises you. Surface that.
