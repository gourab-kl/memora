# Playbook: advisor-brief

**Trigger:** "run the advisor-brief playbook for [advisor name]"

**When to use:** 24 hours before any advisor call, board observer call, or mentor session.

---

## What it does

1. Read any existing notes on this advisor from the vault — their background, past advice, what they're good at, past conversation notes.
2. Read the current company context from `CLAUDE.md` — stage, north star metric, active ventures, current goals.
3. Read the most recent weekly-debrief and any relevant Outputs from the past 30 days.
4. Identify the single most important problem or decision you're facing right now that this advisor is positioned to help with, based on their background.
5. Write a pre-call brief structured as:

   **Context to share with them (2-3 minutes of talk time):**
   What's happened since you last spoke. Metrics, key moves, what changed. Specific and brief — advisors respect founders who can update them quickly.

   **The one ask:**
   The specific question, decision, or problem you want their input on. Be precise. "What should I do about sales?" is not a question. "We're choosing between hiring a VP of Sales now at $180k or promoting our SDR and waiting 6 months — what would you do and why?" is a question.

   **What you've already tried or considered:**
   Show your thinking before asking for theirs. Advisors give better advice when they're building on your reasoning, not starting from scratch.

   **What a useful answer looks like:**
   Not always stated out loud — but know internally whether you want a decision, a framework, an introduction, or just a sanity check. Shape the conversation accordingly.

   **What to listen for:**
   Based on what you know about this advisor's perspective and blind spots, what assumptions in their advice should you probe?

6. Save to `Process/advisors/[advisor]-[date].md` and link to any previous conversation notes.

**Output format:** `Process/advisors/[advisor]-YYYY-MM-DD.md`

**Notes:**
- Advisors are most useful when you come with a sharp question, not an open-ended update.
- Write down what they say during the call. The best advice sounds obvious in the moment and is forgotten by Thursday.
- After the call, add a note to the vault: what was recommended, what you decided to do, what actually happened. This is how advisor relationships compound.
