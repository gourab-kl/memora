# Playbook: partnership-eval

**Trigger:** "run the partnership-eval playbook for [company or partner name]"

**When to use:** When a potential partner, integration, or channel opportunity comes up and you need to decide whether to pursue it.

---

## What it does

1. Read the ICP, GTM motion, north star metric, and active venture goals from `CLAUDE.md`.
2. Search the vault for any existing notes on this company or partner.
3. Evaluate the partnership across five dimensions:

   **Buyer alignment:**
   Does this partner reach our exact ICP — same company size, same role, same pain point? A partnership that reaches adjacent buyers is a distraction, not a shortcut. Score: direct / adjacent / unrelated.

   **Strategic fit:**
   Does this deepen our product value (an integration that makes us stickier) or expand our distribution (a channel that reaches new buyers)? Both are valid — but mixing them up leads to bad deals. Name which one this is.

   **Effort vs. return:**
   What does this actually require from us? Engineering time, dedicated BD hours, revenue share, co-marketing spend? What's the realistic return in the next 12 months — new pipeline, reduced churn, or new market access?

   **Risk:**
   Are we becoming dependent on their platform? Could they build what we do? Is this a competitor in disguise? What happens if they walk away in 12 months?

   **Timing:**
   Are we at the right stage for this partnership? Early-stage companies often take on partnerships that feel like progress but consume the bandwidth needed to close direct customers. Is now the right time?

4. Write a partnership recommendation covering:
   - Pursue / watch / pass — with a clear rationale
   - If pursue: the specific terms to negotiate, the success metric for the first 90 days, and the person to own it
   - If pass: what would have to change for this to be worth revisiting

5. Save to `Process/partnerships/[partner]-[date].md`

**Output format:** `Process/partnerships/[partner]-YYYY-MM-DD.md`

**Notes:**
- Most partnerships feel good and deliver little. The bar for yes should be: would this be one of the top 3 things we did this quarter?
- "We'll figure out the details later" is how bad partnership deals get signed. The details are the deal.
